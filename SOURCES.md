### 2. Divine Names Planetary Correspondences (Step 1.2)

#### Primary Classical Sources

**A. Dalāʾil al-Khayrāt (دلائل الخيرات)**
- **Author**: Imam Muhammad ibn Sulayman al-Jazuli (d. 1465 CE / 869 AH)
- **Full Title**: Dalāʾil al-Khayrāt wa Shawāriq al-Anwār fī Dhikr al-Ṣalāt ʿalā al-Nabī al-Mukhtār
- **Tradition**: Moroccan Shadhili
- **Content**: Divine Names recitation, ṣalawāt on the Prophet ﷺ, spiritual benefits
- **Status**: One of the most widely read Islamic devotional texts worldwide
- **Manuscripts**: Over 1000 surviving manuscripts globally

**B. ʿIlm al-Ḥurūf (علم الحروف) - Science of Letters**
- **Tradition**: Classical Islamic esoteric sciences, Maghribi and Mashriqi traditions
- **Key Texts**: 
  - Kitāb al-Wafq (Book of Magic Squares)
  - Manāzil al-Qamar (Lunar Mansions)
  - Al-Awfāq wa al-Khawāṣṣ (Talismans and Properties)
- **Content**: Letter-number correspondences, planetary associations, elemental connections
- **Scholarly Lineage**: Ibn ʿArabi, al-Buni, al-Tilimsani, and Maghribi scholars

**C. ʿIlm al-Nujūm (علم النجوم) - Science of Celestial Influences**
- **Classical Authorities**: 
  - Al-Biruni (d. 1048 CE)
  - Abū Maʿshar al-Balkhi (d. 886 CE)
  - Ibn Sīnā (Avicenna) - al-Ishārāt sections
- **Content**: Planetary rulerships, elemental theory, temporal influences
- **Islamic Context**: Used for timing of worship and spiritual practices

#### Spiritual Tradition Sources (8 traditions cited)

**1. Shadhili Tradition (الطريقة الشاذلية)**
- **Founder**: Abū al-Ḥasan al-Shadhili (d. 1258 CE)
- **Key Texts**:
  - Ḥizb al-Baḥr (The Litany of the Sea)
  - Ḥizb al-Barr (The Litany of the Land)
  - Wird al-Laṭīf (The Gentle Litany)
  - Wird al-Saḥar (The Dawn Litany)
- **Content**: Divine Name practices, daily awrād (litanies), spiritual development

**2. Tijani Tradition (الطريقة التجانية)**
- **Founder**: Ahmad al-Tijani (d. 1815 CE)
- **Key Texts**:
  - Jawāhir al-Maʿānī (Jewels of Meanings) - Biography and teachings
  - Rimāḥ Ḥizb al-Raḥīm (Spears of the Merciful Party)
  - Ṣalāt al-Fātiḥ commentaries
  - Wird al-Layl (Night Litany)
- **Content**: Specific Divine Name counts, daily wird sequences, spiritual benefits
- **Region**: North and West Africa, widespread global following

**3. Qadiri Tradition (الطريقة القادرية)**
- **Founder**: ʿAbd al-Qadir al-Jilani (d. 1166 CE)
- **Key Texts**:
  - Al-Ghunya li-Ṭālibī Ṭarīq al-Ḥaqq (Sufficient Provision for Seekers of the Path of Truth)
  - Wird al-Ṣaghīr (The Lesser Litany)
  - Futūḥ al-Ghayb (Revelations of the Unseen)
- **Content**: Divine Name practices, spiritual states, daily remembrance

**4. Naqshbandi Tradition (الطريقة النقشبندية)**
- **Founder**: Bahāʾ al-Dīn Naqshband (d. 1389 CE)
- **Content**: Silent dhikr, Divine Name meditation, heart purification
- **Region**: Central Asia, Turkey, South Asia

**5. Shadhili-Darqawi Branch (الدرقاوية)**
- **Founder**: Mawlay al-ʿArabi al-Darqawi (d. 1823 CE)
- **Region**: Morocco, North Africa
- **Content**: Intensive Divine Name recitation, spiritual retreat practices

**6. Shadhili-Alawi Branch (العلوية)**
- **Founder**: Ahmad al-ʿAlawi (d. 1934 CE)
- **Region**: Algeria, North Africa
- **Content**: Divine Name meditation, spiritual education

**7. Moroccan-Shadhili Scholarly Tradition**
- **Key Figures**: Sidi Ahmad al-Badawi, Ibn ʿAjiba, Ahmad Zarruq
- **Content**: Quranic exegesis, Divine Name commentaries, spiritual guidance

**8. West African Scholarly Tradition**
- **Key Texts**:
  - Mawāhib al-Laṭīf (Gifts of the Subtle)
  - Sharḥ Dalāʾil al-Khayrāt (Commentaries on Dalāʾil al-Khayrāt)
  - Various awrād compilations from Senegal, Mali, Mauritania
- **Key Figures**: Shaykh Ibrahim Niasse, Shaykh Ahmadou Bamba, Mauritanian scholars
- **Content**: Abjad numerology (Maghribi system), Divine Name practices, local variations

#### Abjad Numerology System
- **System Used**: Maghribi Abjad (Western Islamic letter-number correspondence)
- **Source**: Classical ʿIlm al-Ḥurūf manuscripts
- **Difference from Mashriqi**: Different ordering of letters (Maghribi vs. Eastern)
- **Application**: Calculating Divine Name values for compatibility and timing

### 3. Post-Prayer Adhkar (Step 1.3)

#### Sunnah Adhkar - Hadith Sources

**A. Sahih Muslim (صحيح مسلم)**
- **Author**: Imam Muslim ibn al-Ḥajjāj (d. 875 CE / 261 AH)
- **Status**: Second most authentic hadith collection in Islam
- **Referenced Hadiths**:
  - **597**: Tasbih (33), Tahmid (33), Takbir (34) after every prayer
  - **811**: Reciting Qul Huwa Allāhu Aḥad (Surah 112)
  - **814**: Reciting Al-Muʿawwidhatayn (Surahs 113-114)
  - **2723**: Morning/Evening protection duʿāʾ (Aṣbaḥnā/Amsaynā)

**B. Sahih al-Bukhari (صحيح البخاري)**
- **Author**: Imam Muhammad al-Bukhari (d. 870 CE / 256 AH)
- **Status**: Most authentic hadith collection in Islam
- **Referenced Hadiths**:
  - **2311**: Ayat al-Kursi after every prayer (protection until next prayer)
  - **6306**: Sayyid al-Istighfār (Master of seeking forgiveness) in morning
  - **6312**: Before-sleep duʿāʾ (Bismika Allāhumma amūtu wa aḥyā)
  - **6369**: Night protection duʿāʾ (from worry, grief, incapacity)

**C. Sunan Abu Dawud (سنن أبي داود)**
- **Author**: Imam Abu Dawud al-Sijistani (d. 889 CE / 275 AH)
- **Status**: One of the six major hadith collections (Kutub al-Sittah)
- **Referenced Hadiths**:
  - **5082**: Al-Muʿawwidhatayn 3x after Fajr and Maghrib for extended protection
  - **1517**: Istighfār after prayer (forgiveness even if fled from battle)

**D. Jami' at-Tirmidhi (جامع الترمذي)**
- **Author**: Imam Muhammad at-Tirmidhi (d. 892 CE / 279 AH)
- **Status**: One of the six major hadith collections
- **Content**: Additional adhkar narrations with authentication grades

**E. Ḥiṣn al-Muslim (حصن المسلم)**
- **Author**: Saʿid ibn Wahf al-Qahtani (contemporary)
- **Full Title**: Ḥiṣn al-Muslim min Adhkār al-Kitāb wa al-Sunnah
- **Content**: Compilation of authentic adhkar from Quran and Sunnah
- **Status**: Widely accepted contemporary reference for daily remembrances
- **Authentication**: All adhkar sourced from Sahih and Hasan hadiths

## Authentication and Scholarly Review

### Levels of Authentication

#### Level 1: Sahih (Authentic) - Highest Authority
- **Content**: All Sunnah adhkar from Sahih Muslim and Sahih al-Bukhari
- **Status**: Universally accepted across all Islamic schools
- **Binding**: Sunnah muʾakkadah (emphasized Sunnah)
- **Examples**: 
  - Tasbih, Tahmid, Takbir after prayer
  - Ayat al-Kursi
  - Al-Muʿawwidhatayn
  - Sayyid al-Istighfār

#### Level 2: Mutawātir Tradition (Mass-Transmitted)
- **Content**: Classical practices transmitted through multiple chains
- **Status**: Widely accepted in traditional Islamic scholarship
- **Binding**: Mustaḥabb (recommended)
- **Examples**:
  - Shadhili awrād (transmitted through thousands of students)
  - Tijani wird (documented chains of transmission)
  - Qadiri practices (established lineages)

#### Level 3: Regional Tradition (Culturally Established)
- **Content**: Practices specific to regional scholarly traditions
- **Status**: Accepted within specific contexts (West African, Moroccan, etc.)
- **Binding**: Mubāḥ (permissible) if within Islamic bounds
- **Examples**:
  - West African Divine Name counts
  - Maghribi abjad practices
  - Local wird variations

#### Level 4: Classical Manuscript Tradition
- **Content**: Planetary hours, celestial timing practices
- **Status**: Requires scholarly verification for contemporary use
- **Binding**: Subject to individual scholarly opinion
- **Examples**:
  - Planetary hour correspondences
  - Specific timing recommendations
  - Esoteric letter sciences (ʿIlm al-Ḥurūf)

### Scholarly Review Checklist

- [ ] **Sunnah Adhkar Review**: All hadith sources verified by qualified muḥaddith (hadith scholar)
- [ ] **Divine Name Practices Review**: Verified that all practices are within Islamic orthodoxy

## Regional Variations

### West African Tradition

#### Characteristics
- **Emphasis**: Tijani and Qadiri awrād dominant
- **Abjad System**: Maghribi letter-number correspondence
- **Language**: Classical Arabic with Ḥassāniyya and Wolof influences
- **Transmission**: Oral transmission alongside written texts
- **Key Centers**: Mauritania, Senegal, Mali, Niger

#### Specific Practices
- **Tijani Wird**: Highly structured daily litanies with specific counts
- **Qadariyya Practices**: Qadiri-Tijani synthesis in some regions
- **Dalāʾil al-Khayrāt**: Widely read, often entire text memorized
- **Khatm Practices**: Group recitation of Divine Names and Quran
- **Mawlid Traditions**: Extensive prophetic praise poetry

#### Key Figures
- **Shaykh Ibrahim Niasse** (d. 1975): Fayḍah Tijāniyyah movement
- **Shaykh Ahmadou Bamba** (d. 1927): Mouride tradition (Senegal)
- **Mauritanian Scholars**: Preservation of classical manuscripts

---

### Moroccan-Maghribi Tradition

#### Characteristics
- **Emphasis**: Shadhili and Jazuli traditions
- **Centers**: Fez (Qarawiyyin), Marrakech, Tétouan
- **Manuscripts**: Extensive library traditions
- **Language**: Classical Arabic with Darija influences

#### Specific Practices
- **Ḥizb al-Baḥr**: Central to Shadhili practice
- **Dalāʾil al-Khayrāt**: Original Moroccan tradition
- **Wird Sequences**: Morning and evening litanies
- **Ziyārah Practices**: Visiting righteous scholars' tombs

#### Key Texts
- **Dalāʾil al-Khayrāt** (original Moroccan manuscript tradition)
- **Al-Minhāj al-Wāḍiḥ** (Clear Method) - Ibn Mashish
- **Ḥizb al-Baḥr** (The Sea Litany) - Abū al-Ḥasan al-Shadhili

---

### Middle Eastern Tradition

#### Characteristics
- **Diversity**: Multiple traditions coexist (Shadhili, Rifai, Naqshbandi, Qadiri)
- **Centers**: Damascus, Cairo, Istanbul, Baghdad
- **Language**: Classical Arabic with regional dialects
- **Scholarship**: Integration of textual and spiritual sciences

#### Specific Practices

**Shadhili (Egypt, Levant):**
- **Ḥizb al-Naṣr** (Litany of Victory)
- **Wird of Imam Abū al-Ḥasan**
- **Cairo-based Shadhili branches**

**Rifai (Iraq, Egypt):**
- **Dhikr circles** with specific movements
- **Divine Name chanting** in gatherings

**Naqshbandi (Turkey, Syria, Caucasus):**
- **Silent dhikr** (khafī)
- **Heart meditation** on Divine Names
- **Rabita** (spiritual connection) practices

## Disclaimers and Limitations

### Important Notes

1. **Not Legal Rulings**: This documentation is for informational and educational purposes. For specific religious rulings (fatāwā), consult qualified scholars.

2. **Regional Context**: Some practices are specific to regional traditions and may not be universal. Users should respect their own tradition's guidelines.

3. **Scholarly Disagreement**: Some practices (especially planetary timing and letter sciences) have scholarly disagreement. Implementation should respect this diversity.

4. **Intention Matters**: All practices must be done with proper Islamic intention (niyyah) - seeking Allah's pleasure alone, not attributing power to creation.

5. **Shirk Warning**: **Critical**: Users must understand that planets, stars, and letters have no independent power. All benefit is from Allah alone. Planetary timing is merely about optimal times for worship, not celestial causation.

6. **Personal Responsibility**: Users are responsible for their own practice and should seek guidance from qualified scholars in their tradition.

### Verification Process

For app deployment, recommended verification:

1. **Hadith Verification**: All Sunnah adhkar cross-referenced with original hadith collections
2. **Manuscript Verification**: Classical practices traced to original manuscripts where possible
3. **Scholarly Consultation**: Review by scholars from relevant traditions
4. **Community Feedback**: Beta testing with practitioners from different traditions
5. **Continuous Review**: Ongoing scholarly oversight and updates
